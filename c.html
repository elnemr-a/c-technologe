The adaptable Lemokey L3 is Keychron's first gaming keyboard.

One of the more well-known brands in the customizable keyboard market is Keychron, but up until this point, it hasn't specifically targeted gamers. The business has established a gaming section called Lemokey, whose first offering is the L3 keyboard, which was crowdfunded. The full-length peripheral supports a 2.4GHz wireless connection with a quick 1,000Hz polling rate, but its true selling point is an aluminum body that is durable and allegedly resilient enough to withstand the rigors of competitive gaming.

You can remap any key, select from three different keycap color schemes, and make macros for both the dedicated macro column and any key command layer. To customize them to your preferences, it is simple to take out and add components. The Lemokey L3 offers Bluetooth 5.1 pairing with up to three devices, but if low latency is your top concern, there is also a cable option.

The Lemokey L3 is anticipated to be delivered by the company in October. A fully constructed kit will require a commitment of at least $209, however the barebones kit (without caps or switches) requires a minimum of $189. The project's financing targets have already been attained.

If you like keyboards with a 60 percent keystroke rate or are simply more concerned with space conservation than a small footprint, the L3 won't be appealing to you. Top-tier esports players might prefer a wired link or a certain design, so this won't necessarily appeal to them. However, for many other players, Lemokey's solution may find a compromise between performance and customisation.
